Video carries emotion and pace, but some things only hold up once you slow down and write them. This blog is that slower place.

## How it relates to the videos

Video pushes an idea in front of you for the first time and gets you to stop and listen. Writing is what survives after you've stopped — what holds up to rereading and to pushback. They aren't two versions of the same thing; they're two links in one chain.

> Only what you've thought through is worth writing down. And only once it's written do you find out where you hadn't actually thought it through.

## What this will cover

- Long-form notes on economics and monetary systems
- Some teardowns: taking rules that look obvious and prying them open to see the assumptions underneath
- The occasional essay

If any of this is useful, follow along on YouTube — new pieces go up there too.
